Meruem says "Then I shall repeat myself" to Palm. It's the first time he's ever thought it courteous to make a point twice. Beforehand, he'd always made a point of not having to repeat himself. It's not exactly subtle, but they don't draw explicit attention to this line either. I take it to mean that he's opened up more to the value of considering others' needs, not just his own.
